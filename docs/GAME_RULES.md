# Bourbonomics

**Welcome to Bourbonomics!**

In *Bourbonomics*, your goal is to become **the Bourbon Baron of America**—a legendary distiller with the most successful bourbon empire! But the road to whiskey wealth isn't easy. You'll need to secure the best ingredients, **outmaneuver competitors**, **manage your bourbon**, and expand your brand while avoiding financial ruin.

Each round, you'll collect resources, **age, and sell bourbon**, navigate shifting market demands, and make strategic investments in your inventory. Will you focus on crafting small-batch artisanal whiskey, or will you scale up for mass production? Can you source prime barrels and top-tier corn? Or will a sudden drop in market demand put your business at risk?

Only the savviest player will rise to the top. **Do you have what it takes to rule the world of bourbon?**

---

## Game Setup

### Game Modes

- **Normal** — First-time players should start with this mode. All instructions are written for the "normal" game mode.
- **Bottled-in-Bond** — This mode is not for the faint of heart; it presents more challenges for the players.

### Winning

- **Triple Crown** — Have 3 Gold Bourbon Awards.
- **Last Baron Standing** — All other players eliminated or bankrupted.
- **Baron of Kentucky** — Have a barrelled bourbon in all 6 rickhouses.

## Board

### Bourbon Regions

The board has the 6 regions of Bourbon. Each region has a **rickhouse** with various slots for bourbon aging.

### Market

The market will always display **5 goods for sale**. These goods are "face-up," so buyers know what they're choosing.

- **Resource cards** — Stored here face-down.
- **Bourbon cards** — Revealed face-up. These are bourbon varieties and move in a "conveyor" fashion depending on the selection criteria.

---

## Turns (Overview)

Each turn includes:

- **Tax** — Pay the bank for your bourbon.
- **Action** — First action is free; then 1, 2, 3… dollars for more actions:
  - Purchase resources (1 from market or 2 random).
  - Sell bourbon.
  - Buy action cards.
  - Trade (free).
- **Barrel** — Barrel bourbon into rickhouses.
- **Market Demand** — Resolved at end of turn (see Phase 4).

## Bourbon 101

### Mash

> **Tip:** The **mash bill** is the specific mix of grains used. By U.S. law, bourbon must be made from at least **51% corn**, with the rest often being **barley, rye, or wheat**. The choice of secondary grains affects the flavor—**rye** adds spice, while **wheat** brings a softer, sweeter profile.

A **mash bill** is the recipe used to make your bourbon. Each mash requires:

1. **1 Cask (30)**
2. **1 Corn (30)**
3. **1 Barley (30)**
4. **At least 1 Wheat or Rye (60)**

**Optional:** Players may add up to **2 additional** Wheat, Rye, or Flavor ingredients to their mash (for 6 cards total). Doing so lets the player access **more Bourbon Cards** during the sale phase.

### Rickhouse

> **Tip:** A **rickhouse** (or rackhouse) is a multi-story warehouse used to age bourbon in barrels. The structure's wood floors, open ventilation, and height all affect how barrels age. Temperature swings across different levels create unique aging conditions—barrels at the top tend to age faster due to higher heat, while barrels at the bottom age slower and smoother.

Rickhouses are where bourbon is stored and aged. There are **6 rickhouses** in the state, each with a capacity of **3, 4, 5, or 6** bourbons.

- Players pay **rent** to the rickhouse based on the total number of barrelled bourbons stored there.
  - **Rent = Total number of barrelled bourbons in that rickhouse.**
- Once a bourbon is barrelled and placed in a rickhouse, it **cannot be moved** to another rickhouse. It can only be moved to **Bottling** when ready.
- If a baron controls **all** the barrelled bourbon in a **5- or 6-capacity** rickhouse, they pay **no rent in future years** for that rickhouse.

**Bourbon Demand** — Bourbon Demand is anywhere from 0–6 barrels. Demand starts the game at **2 barrels**. Each time a mash is sold, demand goes down by **1 barrel**.

### Bourbon Card

Each Bourbon Card has a **Market Price Guide** that shows the **value of your bourbon** based on its **age** and **current market demand**. You won't know its true worth until you go to market.

**How to read the Market Price Guide:** Demand is along the top and Age of your bourbon is along the left side. The value is the cell at the **highest age on the chart that is equal or less than** your bourbon's age, and the **highest demand on the chart that is equal or less than** Market Demand.

- *Example (age):* If you sell a 6-year bourbon and the card shows 2, 4, 8 Years, you sell at the **"4 year"** price.
- *Example (demand):* If market demand is 3 and the card shows 2, 4, 6 market demand, you sell at the **"2 demand"** level.

**Demand is 0:** You may still sell your bourbon for its **age in years** (e.g., a 3-year bourbon sells for 3). Some money is better than no money.

#### Silver Award

If your bourbon qualifies for the **Silver Award**, you may keep the Bourbon Card for **one additional** bourbon sale.

**Note:** You can also sell this Bourbon Card with Silver Award to another player!

#### Gold Award

If your bourbon earns the **Gold Award**, you may use that Bourbon Card for an **unlimited number of barrelled bourbons**—as long as the bourbon you sell meets or exceeds the gold standard.

**Note:** You can also sell this Bourbon Card with the Gold Award to another player!

> **Normal vs Bottled-in-Bond:** In Normal mode, the Gold Award only applies to bourbons you sell using that Bourbon Card. In Bottled-in-Bond mode, the Gold Award applies to **all** bourbons you sell, regardless of whether you're using the Bourbon Card!

## Player Phases (Turn Structure)

### Phase 1: Rickhouse Fees

At the start of their turn, the Baron must pay rent for each rickhouse where they have barrelled bourbon.

1. **Rickhouse Fees**
   - The fee is equal to the **total number of barrelled bourbons** in each rickhouse, including barrels owned by other players.
   - All rickhouse fees must be paid **in full** before the player can continue to the next phase.

2. **Aging Bourbon**
   - For each barrelled bourbon that a player pays rent on, place a **$1 token** (or coin) on top of the barrel.
   - This token represents that the bourbon has **aged one year**.
   - Any additional rent is returned to the bank.

#### Not Enough Cash?

If a player **cannot pay all their rickhouse fees**, the player goes into "bankruptcy":

- The player moves **immediately to Phase 3: Operations**.
  - They do **not** get to age their bourbon (Phase 1), trade or draw resource cards (Phase 2), or barrel bourbon (part of Phase 3).
- The player pays **double the fee** at the **end of their turn** (this includes fees for bourbons that were sold).
- If they **still cannot pay**, the player goes **bankrupt**:
  - All assets are **sold in bulk**—first **resources are auctioned**, then **barrelled bourbons** (auctioned **youngest to oldest**). All money goes to the bank.
  - The player is **eliminated** from the game.

### Phase 2: Preparation

1. **Purchase resources**
   - Players draw **one resource card for free**.
   - Players can purchase additional resource cards for an increasing amount: the first additional card costs **$1**, the next **$2**, then **$3**, and so on.

2. **Trade**
   - Barons can trade resources, their barrelled bourbons, and even their Bourbon Cards in this phase.
   - **Only the current-turn player** can execute a trade. Other players can discuss potential trades, but no transaction occurs until it is their turn.

### Phase 3: Operations

This is the heart of Bourbonomics. During this phase, players can perform the following actions **in any order**, **as many times** as they want (e.g., sell, barrel, sell, barrel).

#### 1. Barrel Bourbon

- Players can **barrel** bourbon into available rickhouses.
- Pay the **entry rent** based on the number of barrelled bourbons (including your new bourbon and other players' bourbons) in that rickhouse.
- Once in the rickhouse, barrelled bourbons **cannot move**. Choose wisely!

#### 2. Sell Bourbon

When selling:

- The player chooses which bourbon they plan to sell (they must **finish the sale** before proceeding with another action).
- Draw **Bourbon Card(s)** depending on their mash bill. Players can draw up to **3 Bourbon Cards** based on their mash bill.
- Choose which Bourbon Card they would like to use.
- If market demand is **0**, the bourbon can still be sold for its **age in years**.

### Phase 4: Market

**Market Demand** is 0–6 barrels. The more barrels, the higher the price your bourbon will sell for.

- Each time a **bourbon is sold**, demand **decreases by 1 barrel**. (e.g., selling 3 bourbons reduces demand by 3.)

At the **end of each turn**, players roll to see how demand shifts:

1. **Roll 2 dice.**
2. **If the dice show different numbers** — Increase demand by **1 barrel** (up to the max of 6).
3. **If the dice are the same (doubles)** — Reduce demand by the **number shown on one of the dice**.
   - *Example:* Double 2s decrease demand by 2; double 5s decrease it by 5.
